The Problem

Multi-Ball Training Ends With
You on the Floor

Every table tennis player who has done multi-ball knows exactly how it ends: basket empties, drill stops, fifteen minutes of crawling around the room. Every single time.

15+ Minutes of Pickup Per Basket

A basket of 100 balls empties in 5–8 minutes. Collecting them all — under the table, in corners, rolled against the wall — eats 15–20 minutes of pure coaching time. Every. Single. Basket.

200+ Times You Bend Down Each Session

Coaching is physically demanding on its own. Add 200 bends under table frames, along baseboards, and into corners — coaches and players wear out fast before training even finishes.

Drill Flow Collapses Every Basket

Student is in the zone, footwork clicking, sequences building — and the basket runs dry. Stop. Collect. The rhythm is gone. That mental state doesn't come back quickly.

Coaching Hours Burned on Janitorial Work

Do the math: 4 sessions a day × 15-minute pickups = 1 hour of a qualified coach's time spent on the floor collecting balls. Every single day. Over 300 hours a year.
